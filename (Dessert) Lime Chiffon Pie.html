Dessert: Lime Chiffon Pie

This is from a '50's era magazine:


"I traveled to Florida resolved to searchout the finest lime pie in the state.  It was in Polk County I found my blue ribbon lime pie perfection.  The baker was Mrs. Thomas Swann, wife of a leading citrus goower, their home, Tara Hall, was in Winter Haven on Lake Eloise.  The pie stood four inches high with peaks of meringue.  The knife cut through the soft filling as smoothly as scissors through silk.  A declicous custard, fragile as spindrift, tart with a zestful zing."    Sounds yummy!!  I wonder if Mom ever tried it.


Lime Chiffon Pie


4 egg yolks

1 15-ounce can sweetened, condensed milk

1/2 cup lime jiuce

1/4 teaspoon salt

6 egg whites

1/2 cup sugar

1 9-inch baked pastry shell


Directions:  Beat four egg yolks until lemon colored; stir in milk, lime juice and salt, blending well.  Beat 6 egg whites until foamy; gradually add sugar and continue beating until meringue stands in peaks.  Fold 1/4 cup of meringue into egg yolk mixture and pour into bake pie shell.  Cover top with remaining meringue.  Bake in a 400 degree oven 8 to 10 minutes, or until lightly browned.  Yield:  6 portions
