Dessert: Peach Cobblers

Mom copied three recipes for Peach Cobbler.  The first two are from the 2004 issue of the Lebanon Church cook book.  The first recipe can use any fruit of your choice.  The last one is Paula Deen's.

 

Cobbler #1

 

1 stick margarine

3/4 c. flour

3/4 c. sugar

3/4 c. milk

1 tsp. baking powder

1/2 tsp. salt

2 c. fruit

 

Melt margarine in pan while preheating oven to 400.  DO NOT STIR.  Make a batter of flour, sugar, milk, baking powder, and salt.  Pour into melted margarine.  Add fruit (such as peeled and cut up fresh peaches).  DO NOT STIR.  

 

Bake at 400 for 40 minutes.

 

 

Peach Cobbler #2

 

Mom noted, "I made this 7-25-05 - too sweet and cooked too long!  My oven is hot!"

 

2 c. fresh or frozen peaches

1 3/4 c. sugar

1 tsp. baking powder

1 stick margarine

1 c. plain flour

1 c. milk

 

Combine peaches and 3/4 cup sugar.  Let stand for 20 minutes.  Melt margarine in 1 1/2 quart baking dish.  Sift flour, 1 c. sugar and baking powder.  Add milk, stir briskly.  Pour into baking dish with margarine.  Top with peaches.  Bake at 350 for 45 minutes.   

 

Paula's Peach Cobbler

 

1 stick butter

1 c. sugar

3/4 c. self-rising flour

3/4 c. milk

1 - 28 oz. can sliced peaches, undrained

 

Preheat oven to 350.  Put butter in dish and put in oven to melt.  Mix sugar and flour; add milk slowly (to prevent lumping).

Pour over melted butter.  Do not stir.  Spoon fruit on top, gently pouring in syrup.  Still do not stir, batter will rise to top during baking.  Bake 30 - 45 minutes.

 

Variation:  Fresh or frozen fruit may be used.  Peel fruit and cut fruit to equal 2 cups.  Add 1 c. sugar and 1 c. water.  Boil, then simmer about 10 minutes until sugar is dissolved.
