Dessert: Pineapple Upside Down Cake

 Pineapple upside-down cake was Dad's favorite.  The first recipe is from Dole; undated.  The second one has its own story!  The third one may be the real keeper.  Mom labeled it as Audrey's cake and commented that it is good!


Pineapple Upside-Down Cake


Mix 1/4 cup melted butter, 3/4 cup brown sugar in cake pan.  Spread the drained DOLE crushed Pineapple evenly over mixture; decorate with cherries and nuts.  Using cake mix or any cake recipe, fill pan 2/3 full.  Bake at 350 for 50 to 60 minutes.  Let stand 5 minutes before turning upside down on plate.  Serve warm with whipped cream.

 

 

 

 


 

Although this recipe isn't dated, it must be from the early 1970's.  During the early days of the microwave, many people thought it would revolutionize the cooking process.  We soon learned you can't cook everything (or much of anything!) in the microwave.  It's a great quick heating device!!  This recipe is included as a remembrance of those days!

 

2 Layer Pineapple Upside Down Cake

 

4 tbsp. butter or margarine

1 c. brown sugar, firmly packed

2 cans (15 1/4 oz. each) sliced pineapple

22 maraschino cherries

1 pkg. (18 1/2 oz.) yellow cake mix


Divide the butter and place in 2 - 8" round microwave safe cake pans.  Microwave one pan at a time on High until the butter melts.  Blend half of the brown sugar with the butter in each pan.  Spread the mixture evenly over the bottom.  Drain pineapple into a measuring cup.  Reserve juice.  Arrange pineapple slices and cherries over the brown sugar mixture.  


Prepare cake mix according to package directions using the pineapple juice as part of the liquid.  Add water to bring level up to the amount called for on the package.  


Pour half the batter over pineapple slices in one pan.  Repeat with the other half of batter in other pan.


Microwave one pan at a time on High for 8 to 10 minutes, turning pan after 2 minutes.  


Invert 1 layer onto serving plate.  Invert the second layer on top of the first.  


Serve warm.
