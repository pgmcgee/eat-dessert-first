Dessert: Stir and Drop Sugar Cookies

This is Janette Ray's recipe.  She was a neighbor of Nana's. Nana made these for Parker and Elizabeth. She had a little bucket for each of them. When Nana and Mom would come to Oviedo to see us she would always fill up their buckets with cookies and give it to them when she arrived. They loved them and then when the Batteiger boys were born I can remember her doing the same thing for them. They are wonderful little bland cookies. Easy to make, too!

 

Stir and Drop Sugar Cookies

 

2 eggs

2/3 cup wesson oil

1 tsp vanilla

3/4 cup sugar

2 cups flour (sifted)

2 tsp baking powder

1/2 tsp salt

 

Heat oven to 400. Beat eggs with fork until well blended, stir in wesson oil and vanilla. Blend in sugar until it thickens. Add dry ingredients. Add to egg mixture. Drop by tsp full onto ungreased cookie sheet 2 inches apart. Dip bottom of glass in oil, dip in sugar and flatten cookie. Bake 8-10 minutes.
